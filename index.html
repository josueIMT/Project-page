GCD: Geometry-Constrained Pattern Reasoning for Text-Driven 3D Hand–Object Motion Synthesis.

ICPR 2026 Conference submission

1IMT Nord Europe, Lille, FRANCE

Abstract

Generating physically plausible hand-object interactions from textual descriptions remains a challenging problem. Although diffusion-based motion models capture semantic intent and kinematic variability, they often lack explicit geometric reasoning during denoising, leading to weak contact and severe interpenetration. We introduce GCD, a geometry-constrained dual-stream diffusion framework that embeds geometric awareness directly into the latent denoising trajectory. GCD first predicts a 3D contact probability field from the object mesh and text, providing spatial priors for interaction hotspots. Motion synthesis is then guided by two complementary conditioning pathways: an object stream that encodes global semantic intent, and a hand stream that fuses textual cues, object features, and the predicted contact field to align motion with local geometric structure. To further ensure geometric plausibility, GCD incorporates two dedicated modules. A Geometry Predictor extracts timestep-dependent geometry embeddings and estimates penetration likelihoods from noisy hand–object trajectories. A Geometry Corrector refines latent features through gated, geometry-aware modulation. Together, these modules impose a differentiable, timestep-aware geometric constraint throughout denoising, improving contact stability and substantially reducing penetration artifacts.
